Standard Operating Procedure

Asthma QOF
2025/26

Achieving targets for AST012 & AST007 — a guide for administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
Asthma is a Preventable Cause of Death Asthma causes approximately 1,200 deaths in the UK each year — the majority of which are considered preventable. The National Review of Asthma Deaths (NRAD) found that most deaths were associated with suboptimal care, poor patient self-management, and failure to recognise deteriorating control. Robust diagnosis, regular annual reviews, and personalised action plans directly save lives.
ℹ️
Scope This SOP covers two QOF indicators: AST012 (objective testing at new diagnosis) and AST007 (annual asthma review). Both apply to patients on the Asthma register. Children under 5 are excluded from AST007. This SOP is aligned to the new combined BTS/NICE/SIGN asthma guideline (NG245, November 2024).
1

QOF Indicators at a Glance

Code Indicator Points Target Window
AST012 Patients with a new diagnosis of asthma on or after 1 April 2025 with a record of an objective test between 3 months before or 3 months after diagnosis 15 45–80% ±3 months of diagnosis (93 days before)
AST007 Patients with asthma who have had an annual review including: asthma control assessment, number of exacerbations recorded, inhaler technique assessed, and written personalised action plan provided — all coded on the same day as the review 20 45–70% Preceding 12 months
💡
AST012 — Updated for 2025/26 (replaces AST011) Reflects the new BTS/NICE/SIGN combined guideline (NG245, November 2024). The indicator aligns to the recommended objective testing sequence. The full range of possible objective tests counts as a success — it is not limited to a single test type.

Objective Testing at New Diagnosis (AST012)

A combination of a suggestive clinical history and a supporting objective test is required to diagnose asthma. NG245 recommends a specific sequence, with different pathways for adults/young people (over 16) and children aged 5–16. Any test in the sequence that is recorded within the window counts.

🧑 Adults & Young People (aged >16)
1
Blood eosinophil count OR FeNO
Diagnose if eosinophil count above lab reference range, or FeNO ≥50 ppb
2
Bronchodilator reversibility (BDR) with spirometry
If step 1 inconclusive. Diagnose if FEV1 increase ≥12% & ≥200 ml from pre-bronchodilator, or ≥10% of predicted normal FEV1
3
Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF) variability
If spirometry unavailable or delayed: PEF twice daily for 2 weeks. Diagnose if variability (amplitude % mean) ≥20%
4
Bronchial challenge test
If all above inconclusive but asthma still clinically suspected — refer for consideration. Diagnose if bronchial hyper-responsiveness present.
🧒 Children & Young People (aged 5–16)
1
FeNO
Diagnose if FeNO ≥35 ppb
2
Bronchodilator reversibility (BDR) with spirometry
If FeNO not raised or unavailable. Diagnose if FEV1 increase ≥12% from baseline or ≥10% of predicted normal FEV1
3
Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF) variability
If spirometry unavailable or delayed: PEF twice daily for 2 weeks. Diagnose if variability ≥20%
4
Skin prick test / total IgE + eosinophil count
If above inconclusive: sensitisation to house dust mite or raised total IgE and eosinophil count >0.5×10⁹/L
5
Bronchial challenge test (paediatric specialist)
If still uncertain — refer to paediatric specialist for consideration.
📋
Cannot Perform Tests? If a patient aged 5 or over cannot perform objective tests, treat empirically with ICS, review regularly, and retry tests every 6–12 months. PCA exception codes are available where the patient declines, does not attend, or objective testing is not appropriate or feasible. Asthma in children under 5 is not captured by AST012.
4

Annual Asthma Review — AST007

Use the (RP) LTC Review Template CEG v1.4 in EMIS. Children under 5 are excluded from this indicator.

⚠️
AST007 — All four components must be coded on the same day as the review QOF business rules require the asthma review, the number of exacerbations, the inhaler technique assessment, and the written personalised asthma action plan to all be recorded on the same date. Coding any element on a different day means the indicator will not be met for that patient. Build all four into a single template/workflow.
📊
1. Assessment of Asthma Control Use the Asthma Control Test (ACT). Code the score and date on the day of the review.
🗓️
2. Number of Exacerbations Recorded Record the number of exacerbations in the month before the review. Must be coded on the same day as the review — not retrospectively or on a different date.
💨
3. Inhaler Technique Assessment Document whether technique is satisfactory and record any corrective advice given. Code on the day of the review appointment.
📄
4. Written Personalised Asthma Action Plan Provide a written personalised asthma action plan and code its provision on the same day as the review. Review and update existing plans — do not carry forward an out-of-date plan.
📋  Additional Review Components (Best Practice, NG245)
Medicines Adherence

Check adherence using prescription records. Non-adherence is a common cause of apparent poor control — explore before stepping up treatment.

Triggers & Risk Factors

Check smoking status, occupational exposures, psychosocial factors, and seasonal or environmental triggers.

No SABA Without ICS

SABAs must not be prescribed without a concomitant ICS for any patient with asthma of any age (NG245 1.6.3). Flag to Pharmacist/GP if identified.

💡
When to Refer — Pharmacological Escalation Aged ≥12: refer to an asthma specialist if not controlled on high-dose ICS treatment (NG245 1.7.11). Aged 5–11: refer if not controlled on paediatric moderate-dose MART or ICS/LABA ± LTRA (NG245 1.8.7). NG245 pharmacological management Algorithms C (≥12 years) and D (5–11 years) are published at nice.org.uk/guidance/ng245/resources.

Step-by-Step Process

1
Register Management

The GP Lead reviews the asthma register at a minimum annually and opportunistically throughout the year. An accurate register is the foundation of all QOF attainment.

  • Add patients with confirmed new asthma diagnoses promptly
  • Remove patients where asthma diagnosis has been excluded or was incorrectly coded
  • Review patients with diagnostic uncertainty — confirm objective testing is complete or outstanding
  • Children under 5 may be on the register but are excluded from AST007
2
New Diagnosis — Objective Testing (AST012)

At the time of new asthma diagnosis (on or after 1 April 2025), an objective test must be ordered and the result coded within 3 months before or 3 months after the date of diagnosis (93 days before).

  • GP makes clinical diagnosis and codes it in EMIS — this triggers the AST012 window
  • Order the appropriate first-line test: FeNO or blood eosinophils (adults/>16); FeNO (children 5–16)
  • If spirometry needed and unavailable in-house: send referral to community spirometry service; document referral
  • Admin diarises the 3-month deadline from the date of diagnosis coding
  • When result received: code objective test result in EMIS on the date of receipt
  • If patient unable or declines: GP approves PCA exception code with documented justification
3
Identify Patients Due an Annual Review (AST007)

Admin can identify and recall patients due a review at any time using the QOF dashboard — they do not need to wait for the monthly QOF meeting. The monthly meeting is used to review progress, problem-solve barriers, and manage complex cases such as persistent non-attenders.

  • Identify patients with no review in the past 12 months
  • Identify patients with incomplete reviews — missing any of the four required components
  • Identify patients with outstanding objective test results from new diagnoses (AST012)
  • Admin identifies patients due a review from the QOF dashboard and begins recall without waiting for the monthly QOF meeting
4
Invite & Book
  • Admin sends personalised invitation letter or SMS to patients identified as due a review — on an ongoing basis, without waiting for the monthly QOF meeting
  • Book annual asthma review with Asthma Nurse/HCA — 20–30 minutes; advise patient to bring inhalers
  • Book follow-up Pharmacist appointment if medication review is required
  • No response after 2 weeks: send second invitation. After a further 2 weeks: telephone outreach.
  • Still no response: add to monthly QOF meeting agenda for team review
5
Conduct the Annual Review

Asthma Nurse or HCA conducts the review using (RP) LTC Review Template CEG v1.4. All four QOF components must be coded on the same day.

  • Complete asthma control assessment (ACT/ACQ/C-ACT) and code score on the day
  • Record number of exacerbations in the preceding period — code on the same day
  • Observe inhaler technique with patient's own inhaler — document and code on the same day
  • Provide, review and update written personalised asthma action plan — code on the same day
  • Check adherence via prescription records; explore and address non-adherence before escalating
  • Check smoking status, occupational triggers, environmental and seasonal factors
  • Confirm no SABA is prescribed without concomitant ICS — flag to Pharmacist/GP if found
  • Flag patients with uncontrolled asthma or meeting referral criteria to GP
6
Medication Review (Pharmacist)

Arranged for patients with medication-related concerns identified at the annual review, or where complex pharmacological management is in place.

  • Review current inhaler regimen against NG245 pharmacological algorithms (C & D)
  • Identify SABA prescribed without ICS; correct with GP oversight
  • Review inhaler device for patient preference, technique, and environmental impact
  • Assess LABA, LTRA, MART regimens for appropriateness
  • Identify patients meeting specialist referral criteria and flag to GP same day
7
Did Not Attend (DNA) Management
  • First DNA: re-invite by letter or SMS within 5 working days; note attempt in EMIS
  • Second DNA: telephone contact attempt; document outcome
  • Third DNA / no response: add to monthly QOF meeting agenda for team review
  • GP reviews repeated non-attenders; considers clinical vulnerability and appropriate next steps
  • Where exception reporting is appropriate: GP approves PCA code with documented justification
6

Exception Reporting

Patient Consent Adjustments (PCAs) allow practices to exclude individual patients from QOF indicators where it would be clinically inappropriate or impossible to include them. All exception reporting must be approved by the GP and documented with a clear clinical or administrative reason.

🩺  Clinical Exceptions
  • Patient unable to perform objective tests (e.g. severe breathlessness) — treat empirically with ICS and retry every 6–12 months
  • Objective testing clinically contraindicated or not appropriate — must be documented with reasoning
  • Terminal illness or severe comorbidity making annual review clinically inappropriate
  • Patient registered within the preceding 3 months (new patient exception)
  • Asthma diagnosis in doubt / under review — objective testing pathway ongoing
📋  Administrative Exceptions
  • Patient declines the annual review or objective test despite invitation (patient declined)
  • Patient does not attend after a minimum of two invitation attempts (DNA)
  • AST012: objective testing not feasible within the 3-month window (e.g. patient acutely unwell, community service unavailable)
  • AST012: patient declines or does not attend for objective testing
⚠️
Exception reporting must never be used to improve QOF scores without clinical justification. Commissioners may audit a random sample of exceptions — incorrect or unjustified coding can result in financial claw-back. Always document the reason clearly in the patient record before applying a PCA.
7

Monitoring & QOF Meeting

📅
Monthly QOF Meeting The GP Lead, Pharmacist, Practice Manager and Admin Lead meet monthly to review the QOF dashboard, assess performance against AST007 and AST012 targets, problem-solve barriers (e.g. DNA patterns, coding errors, spirometry capacity), and agree actionable improvement plans for the following month.
Frequency Action Responsible
Monthly Monthly QOF meeting — review AST007 & AST012 dashboard; agree recall actions; review DNA list; approve exception coding GP Lead (Chair)
Monthly Admin identifies patients due a review from the QOF dashboard and sends invites on an ongoing basis — does not wait for the monthly QOF meeting Admin Lead / PM
Monthly New diagnoses reviewed — confirm objective test ordered and coded within 3-month window; check any outstanding deadlines (AST012) Asthma Nurse / Admin
Monthly Pharmacist reviews patients with medication concerns identified at annual reviews — SABA monotherapy, step-up requirements, referral criteria Pharmacist
As needed DNA or declining patients reviewed at monthly QOF meeting — team agrees approach; GP approves exception coding where appropriate QOF Meeting
Quarterly Mid-year attainment review — assess trajectory against 45–80% (AST012) and 45–70% (AST007); adjust recall intensity if required PM / GP Lead
Annually Full register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, remove incorrect entries, validate coding GP Lead
Annually SOP review — update in line with any changes to NHS England QOF guidance or BTS/NICE/SIGN NG245 GP Lead / PM

Admin & Clinical Checklist

NEW DIAGNOSIS — AST012 Checklist

  • ✓New asthma diagnosis coded in EMIS by GP — date of diagnosis recorded (opens 3-month AST012 window)
  • ✓Objective test ordered at time of diagnosis per age-specific pathway (FeNO/eosinophils for adults; FeNO for children 5–16)
  • ✓If spirometry required and unavailable in-house: referral sent to community spirometry service; referral documented in EMIS
  • ✓Admin diarises 3-month deadline (93 days from diagnosis) for objective test result to be coded
  • ✓Objective test result received and coded in EMIS on the date of receipt — within the 3-month window
  • ✓If patient unable or declines testing: PCA exception approved by GP and clearly documented in record

ANNUAL REVIEW — AST007 Checklist (per patient)

  • ✓Patient identified as due a review from QOF dashboard — admin initiates recall
  • ✓Invitation letter/SMS sent within 5 working days (date logged in EMIS)
  • ✓Annual asthma review booked with Asthma Nurse/HCA — 20–30 minutes; patient advised to bring inhalers
  • ✓Review attended — all four QOF components coded on the same day using (RP) LTC Review Template CEG v1.4
  • ✓Adherence checked via prescription records; non-adherence explored and documented
  • ✓No SABA without concomitant ICS confirmed — flagged to Pharmacist/GP if found
  • ✓If Pharmacist medication review required: follow-up appointment booked
  • ✓If referral criteria met: GP informed same day
  • ✓If DNA: second invite sent and further contact attempts logged
  • ✓If persistent DNA/declining: added to monthly QOF meeting agenda; GP approves exception code if appropriate
