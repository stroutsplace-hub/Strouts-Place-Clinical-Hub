Standard Operating Procedure

CHD Secondary
Prevention 2025/26

Achieving targets for CHD005, CHD015 & CHD016 — a guide for administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
The Leading Cause of Premature Death in the UK Coronary heart disease is the single most common cause of premature death in the UK. For patients with established CHD, evidence-based secondary prevention — antiplatelet therapy and blood pressure control — significantly reduces the risk of further events, hospitalisation and death.
ℹ️
Scope All indicators apply to patients on the CHD register. This SOP covers the full annual review pathway — from register identification through to clinical review, medication review, BP optimisation, coding, and QOF monitoring.

⚠️
CHD015 / CHD016 — The last reading counts QOF uses the most recent BP reading in the preceding 12 months. Repeat readings taken at Pharmacist BP clinic follow-up become the last reading on record — ensuring timely follow-up is critical to QOF attainment.
💡
CHD005 — What counts? Aspirin, clopidogrel, ticagrelor, prasugrel, dipyridamole (alone or combined), warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban, or dabigatran. The medication must be confirmed as actively being taken — not merely prescribed. Confirmation is completed by the Pharmacist at the medication review appointment.
💊  Antiplatelet Therapy — Clinical Guidance
Long-term / Stable CHD

Long-term antiplatelet therapy is recommended. Aspirin (75 mg daily) is first line; clopidogrel 75 mg daily is the usual alternative where aspirin is contraindicated or not tolerated.

After an Acute CHD Event

Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) may be recommended following ACS or PCI. This should revert to monotherapy within 6–12 months in line with specialist recommendation. Review the discharge summary and cardiology letters to confirm the intended duration.

Gastroprotection

Most older patients on long-term antiplatelet therapy will benefit from gastroprotection with a PPI. Review at the medication review appointment and prescribe if not already in place.

Roles & Responsibilities

🗂️
Practice Manager
  • Owns overall QOF performance
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting; reviews dashboard and agrees action plans
  • Coordinates recall campaign with admin team following each QOF meeting
  • Chairs escalation of persistent non-responders at the monthly QOF meeting
📋
Admin / Reception Team
  • Sends invitation letters and SMS when directed following the monthly QOF meeting
  • Books annual CHD review with HCA — 20 minutes
  • Books follow-up Pharmacist appointment once annual review is complete
  • Scans and codes results received from secondary care on receipt
  • Logs DNA attempts and adds patients with repeated non-response to the monthly QOF meeting agenda
🩺
Healthcare Assistant (HCA)
  • Conducts annual CHD review using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template in EMIS
  • Records blood pressure (clinic or HBPM) with method documented
  • Records weight, BMI, smoking status and alcohol (AUDIT-C)
  • Calculates and codes QRISK3 score
  • Offers HBPM to patients with borderline or elevated clinic readings
  • Codes all findings on the day of the appointment
  • Flags uncontrolled BP to Pharmacist — or to GP same day if BP ≥180/120
💊
Pharmacist
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting with GP Lead, PM and Admin Lead
  • Conducts medication review at the follow-up appointment
  • Confirms antiplatelet / anticoagulant therapy is being taken and codes CHD005
  • Reviews antihypertensive regimen and optimises BP for patients not at target
  • Arranges monitoring investigations as appropriate — e.g. renal function, liver function, urine ACR
  • Runs Pharmacist BP clinic follow-up appointments
  • Escalates to GP if clinical concerns are identified
👨‍⚕️
GP
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting; chairs review
  • Acts on clinical concerns escalated by any clinician
  • Same-day review of any patient with BP ≥180/120 mmHg
  • Approves clinical exception reporting
  • Annual register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, remove incorrect entries
  • Reviews patients with repeated DNA / declining attendance raised at the monthly QOF meeting

Step-by-Step Process

1
Identify Patients Due a Review

The QOF dashboard and CHD register are reviewed at the monthly QOF meeting by the GP Lead, Pharmacist and Practice Manager. Admin do not generate reports independently — recall lists are agreed at the QOF meeting and passed to the admin team for action.

  • Review CHD register at QOF meeting — identify patients with no review in the past 12 months or an outstanding indicator
  • Identify patients whose last BP reading exceeds the age-appropriate target (CHD015 / CHD016)
  • Identify patients with no antiplatelet / anticoagulant confirmed as taken in the past 12 months (CHD005)
  • Admin team notified at or following the QOF meeting of patients to recall
2
Invite & Book
  • Admin sends a personalised invitation letter or SMS within 5 working days of the QOF meeting
  • Book an annual CHD review with HCA — 20 minutes
  • Advise patient to bring a list of current medications to the appointment
  • Book a follow-up appointment with the Pharmacist to take place after the annual review is complete
  • If no response after 2 weeks: send a second invitation. After a further 2 weeks: telephone outreach.
  • If still no response after further attempts: add to the monthly QOF meeting agenda for the team to review
3
Annual CHD Review — HCA

The HCA completes all physical checks using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template in EMIS. Everything must be coded on the same day.

❤️🩺  Annual CHD review — all checks
  • ✓Blood pressure — clinic reading (CHD015 / CHD016)
  • ✓Weight & BMI
  • ✓QRISK3 calculated and coded
  • ✓Smoking status
  • ✓Alcohol status (AUDIT-C)
  • ✓Offer HBPM if clinic BP borderline or elevated
  • ✓All findings coded same day in EMIS
🔁  HCA — BP flagging at end of appointment
⚠️  BP above target but <180/120

Flag to Pharmacist on the day. Admin to book a TC or F2F with the Pharmacist within 1 week.

🚨  BP ≥180/120

Do not book routine Pharmacist follow-up. Inform the GP same day for immediate discussion and review.

4
Medication Review & BP Optimisation — Pharmacist

Once the annual review is complete, the Pharmacist conducts a follow-up appointment (TC or F2F, within 1 week where BP is above target) to complete the medication review, confirm CHD005, and optimise BP.

  • CHD005: Confirm with the patient that antiplatelet or anticoagulant therapy is being actively taken and code the medication status. If therapy is not being taken and there is no recorded contraindication, initiate a prescription or escalate to the GP.
  • BP above target (CHD015 / CHD016): Review antihypertensive regimen and adherence; intensify therapy (add or up-titrate) as clinically appropriate; arrange Pharmacist BP clinic follow-up to recheck BP after medication change
  • Arrange monitoring investigations as appropriate — e.g. renal function, liver function, urine ACR — particularly following medication initiation or dose change
  • Signpost to lifestyle interventions: smoking cessation, weight management, alcohol reduction, physical activity
  • Escalate to GP if clinical concerns are identified
🚨
BP ≥180/120 mmHg — GP same-day review A reading of ≥180/120 mmHg at the HCA appointment bypasses the routine Pharmacist pathway. The HCA informs the GP on the same day for immediate discussion and review. The GP assesses for hypertensive urgency / emergency and determines the appropriate management and follow-up plan.
🩺
Pharmacist BP Clinic Follow-up Where BP remains above the age-appropriate target following medication change, the Pharmacist schedules a dedicated BP clinic follow-up. The most recent BP reading in the 12-month window determines QOF attainment. Allow sufficient time before the QOF year-end (31 March) for this cycle to complete.
📋  LES — High-intensity Statin (Pharmacist awareness)

The LES requires that patients with established CVD — including CHD, stroke/TIA, and peripheral arterial disease (PAD) — are prescribed a high-intensity statin.

Qualifying statins
  • ·Atorvastatin 40 mg
  • ·Atorvastatin 80 mg
  • ·Rosuvastatin 20 mg
Pharmacist action
  • ·Check current statin and dose at medication review
  • ·Up-titrate or switch to qualifying statin where appropriate
  • ·Document reason if high-intensity statin not prescribed

Where statin is declined or not tolerated, document the reason and consider an alternative lipid-lowering agent. Escalate to GP if clinically uncertain.

5
Coding & Documentation
  • All findings coded in EMIS on the day of the appointment using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template
  • BP readings: use correct SNOMED codes and distinguish clinic readings from HBPM readings
  • CHD005: antiplatelet / anticoagulant coded as being taken following Pharmacist confirmation at the medication review
  • Secondary care correspondence: admin to scan and code relevant results and medication changes on receipt
  • Verify patient age band at each review — patients may cross the ≤79 / ≥80 threshold between cycles; apply the correct indicator
6
Exception Reporting

Exceptions may be entered by any appropriate member of staff but must be approved by the GP, and only where clinically justified.

  • Patient has been informed of the recommendation but declines (patient declined)
  • Clinical contraindication documented and approved by the GP (e.g. antiplatelet therapy contraindicated)
  • Patient recently registered (within 3 months) — new patient exception
  • Maximum tolerated antihypertensive therapy already in use and target not achievable
⚠️
Exception reporting must never be used to improve QOF scores without clinical justification. Commissioners may audit a random sample of exceptions — incorrect coding can result in financial claw-back.
4

Monitoring & QOF Meeting

📅
Monthly QOF Meeting The GP Lead, Pharmacist, Practice Manager and Admin Lead meet monthly to review the QOF dashboard, assess performance against CHD005, CHD015 and CHD016, problem-solve barriers and agree an actionable improvement plan. This meeting is also the forum for reviewing patients who have not responded to recall — the team agrees the approach collectively.
Frequency Activity Owner
Monthly QOF meeting — GP Lead, Pharmacist, PM and Admin Lead review CHD dashboard, identify patients to recall, review non-responders and agree action plan GP Lead (Chair)
Monthly Admin team informed of patients to invite for annual review following QOF meeting Practice Manager / Admin Lead
Monthly Pharmacist reviews patients with BP above target — confirms Pharmacist BP clinic follow-up is booked Pharmacist
As needed Patients with DNA or declining attendance reviewed at monthly QOF meeting — team agrees approach; GP involved only if clinical concerns QOF Meeting
Annually Full register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, remove incorrect entries, validate age-band splits and coding GP Lead
