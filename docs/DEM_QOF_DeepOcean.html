Standard Operating Procedure

Dementia
QOF 2025/26

Achieving the DEM004 target — annual dementia care plan review — a guide for all administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
A Major and Growing Challenge Dementia affects approximately 7% of adults over 65 and is one of the leading causes of late-life disability. Alzheimer's disease accounts for 50–75% of cases. Regular structured reviews are proven to identify changing needs early and reduce crisis presentations. Failing to review carries direct patient harm.
ℹ️
Scope DEM004 applies to all patients on the dementia register, regardless of dementia subtype. This SOP covers the full recall and review pathway — from register identification through to care plan review, carer assessment, coding, and QOF monitoring.
1

QOF Indicator at a Glance

Code Indicator Points Target Window
DEM004 The percentage of patients diagnosed with dementia whose care plan has been reviewed in the preceding 12 months 14 35–70% 12 months
💡
DEM004 — What counts as a review? The review must be face-to-face or by telephone/video (in line with patient preference). It must address: physical & mental health assessment, medication review, legal/welfare safeguards, and carer needs. Where no care plan yet exists, one must be created at this appointment.

Roles & Responsibilities

🗂️
Practice Manager
  • Owns overall DEM004 QOF performance
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting to review dashboard and agree actions
  • Coordinates recall campaign with admin lead
  • Escalates persistent non-responders to GP lead
  • Ensures carers and proxy contacts are up to date in EMIS
📋
Admin / Reception Team
  • Generates dementia register reports monthly
  • Sends invitation letters and SMS recall — include carer/proxy if consent on file
  • Books GP or Pharmacist-led review appointment
  • Records all contact attempts and DNA outcomes in EMIS
  • Refers newly diagnosed patients for first review within 6 months of diagnosis
🩺
GP
  • Conducts the structured dementia care plan review
  • Documents review with correct SNOMED code same day using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template
  • Considers palliative care register addition as appropriate
  • Manages any acute clinical issues identified at review
  • Reviews and updates care plan or advance care plan in record
💊
Pharmacist
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting with GP lead, PM and admin lead
  • May conduct the full dementia care plan review using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template
  • Reviews anticholinergic burden and ongoing antipsychotic use
  • Checks NICE-recommended dementia medications are appropriate (CEIs, Memantine)
  • Documents medication review findings in EMIS
3

Recall & Review Process

1
Identify Patients Due for Review

Admin runs the dementia register report at the start of each month. Flag all patients whose annual care plan review is due or overdue within the next 8 weeks. Cross-reference with any patients newly diagnosed in the past 6 months who have not yet had a first review.

2
Send Invitation

Send a combined patient and carer invitation (where consent is on file) by letter and/or SMS. Invite the carer or named key worker to attend. Allow 2 weeks for response before follow-up contact.

  • Record date and method of all contact attempts in EMIS
  • If no response after 2 weeks, telephone patient and/or carer
  • If housebound or care home resident, arrange home visit or care home liaison
3
Book & Prepare Appointment

Book an appointment with the GP or designated clinician. Where patient preference is telephone or video, this is entirely acceptable. Ensure the EMIS record is open and the previous care plan / advance care plan is accessible before the appointment begins.

  • Confirm carer / key worker attendance or availability by phone on day
  • Flag appointment type as dementia annual review in EMIS for QOF extraction
4
Conduct the Structured Review

The clinician works through all four mandatory domains of the care plan review (see Section 4 — Care Plan Review Domains). Document each domain addressed and any actions or onward referrals. Focus review on the patient's and carer's priorities.

  • Physical health assessment — including behavioural change screening
  • Medication review — CEIs, Memantine, antipsychotics, anticholinergics
  • Legal & welfare — LPA, end of life preferences, palliative care register
  • Carer assessment — impact of caring, access to benefits, carer health check offer
5
Code & Update the Record

Apply the correct SNOMED code for dementia care plan review on the day of the appointment using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template in EMIS. Update or create the care plan or advance care plan in the EMIS record. Document all actions, referrals, and shared decisions made during the review.

  • SNOMED: Dementia annual review — code same day
  • Record named coordinator / key worker and contact details
  • Record legal power of attorney status
  • Record end of life preferences if discussed
  • Consider adding to palliative care register if appropriate
6
Manage Did Not Attend (DNA)

If patient does not attend, a second invitation should be sent within 5 working days. Attempt telephone contact with patient and carer/proxy. If the patient is in a care home, liaise directly with the care home key worker. After three failed contacts, escalate to the monthly QOF meeting for team review and consider whether a home visit or exception reporting is appropriate.

  • DNA 1: Second invite within 5 working days
  • DNA 2: Phone attempt — patient and carer/proxy
  • DNA 3: Escalate to monthly QOF meeting
4

Care Plan Review — Mandatory Domains

All four domains must be addressed and documented at each annual review. Commissioner verification audits will check for evidence of each domain. Reviews should be patient-centred and geared to the patient's and carer's current priorities.

🫁 Physical & Mental Health Assessment
  • Physical health and symptom review — including joint pain and inter-current infections as causes of behavioural change
  • Screen for new dementia symptoms: wandering, delusions, hallucinations
  • Screen for depression (more common in dementia patients)
  • Assess for anxiety and apathy
  • Social care needs and referrals as appropriate
  • Review NICE-recommended non-pharmacological interventions — cognitive stimulation therapy (CST) offered / ongoing
💊 Medication Review
  • Review NICE-recommended dementia medications:
    — Cholinesterase inhibitors (CEIs): donepezil, galantamine, rivastigmine — for Alzheimer's, DLB, PDD
    — Memantine — consider for moderate/severe Alzheimer's
  • Review any ongoing antipsychotic medication — document justification and consider side effects: CVD risk, diabetes, falls
  • Review all medications with anticholinergic effects — assess cumulative burden
  • Document if medications declined or unsuitable
⚖️ Legal, Welfare & End of Life
  • Confirm or update Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) — health & welfare and/or property & financial affairs; document if not in place
  • Review and record end of life preferences — ensure patient and carer understand how to access palliative care at the appropriate time
  • Consider adding patient to palliative care register if appropriate
  • Record named coordinator / key worker and their contact details
  • Confirm patient and carer are accessing all appropriate benefits
🤝 Carer Assessment & Support
  • Identify the patient's carer(s) and record in EMIS
  • Confirm consent to communicate with carer(s) — document in record
  • Assess the impact of caring on the carer — note that male carers are less likely to volunteer distress
  • Offer a carer health check — if carer is registered at a different practice, advise them to contact their own GP
  • Provide details of support services and address carer's information needs based on illness stage
  • Include carer in care plan / advance care plan discussions
  • Offer carer psychoeducation — document if offered and whether accepted
🚨
Antipsychotic Prescribing Alert Antipsychotic medications in dementia patients carry significant risks including increased stroke risk, accelerated cognitive decline, and falls. Each review must explicitly justify continued use, document a plan to reduce or stop where possible, and record discussion of current and future side effects with the patient and/or carer.
🧠
Anticholinergic Burden Many commonly prescribed medications carry anticholinergic effects (e.g. bladder medications, some antihistamines, tricyclics). These are particularly harmful in dementia patients. Use the ACB (Anticholinergic Cognitive Burden) calculator and document the review of all relevant medications at each annual review.

Monitoring & Governance

📅
Monthly QOF Meeting The GP Lead, Pharmacist, Practice Manager and Admin Lead meet monthly to review the QOF dashboard, assess DEM004 performance against the 35–70% target range, problem-solve barriers and agree actionable improvement plans for the following month.
Frequency Activity Owner
Monthly QOF meeting — GP Lead, Pharmacist, PM and Admin Lead review DEM004 dashboard, performance against threshold and agree action plans GP Lead (Chair)
Monthly Run dementia register report; identify patients due for review and book appointments Admin Lead
Annually Full register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, validate existing entries, remove incorrect coding GP Lead
As needed Patients with 3+ failed contacts or DNAs escalated to monthly QOF meeting for team review; consider exception reporting with documented justification Admin Lead → QOF Meeting
