Standard Operating Procedure

COPD QOF
2025/26

Achieving targets for COPD010 — a guide for administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
COPD Is a Major Cause of Preventable Morbidity and Mortality COPD is a common, progressive and disabling condition responsible for significant unscheduled healthcare use — including emergency admissions and prolonged inpatient stays. The most impactful interventions — annual flu vaccination and tobacco dependence treatment — are delivered in primary care. Structured annual reviews with MRC dyspnoea grading and exacerbation recording are the foundation of personalised management plans and help prevent avoidable deterioration.
ℹ️
Scope This SOP covers one QOF indicator: COPD010 — the annual review for all patients on the COPD register. It covers the full pathway from register management through to clinical review, coding, exception reporting, and QOF monitoring. This SOP is aligned to NICE NG115 (Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease in over 16s: diagnosis and management) and NICE IND191 (the basis for COPD010).
1

QOF Indicator at a Glance

Code Indicator Points Target Window
COPD010 Patients with COPD who have had a review in the preceding 12 months including: a record of the number of exacerbations and an assessment of breathlessness using the MRC dyspnoea scale 9 50–90% Preceding 12 months
⚠️
COPD010 — Both Components Must Be Recorded at the Same Review The number of exacerbations and the MRC dyspnoea scale score must both be recorded as part of the annual review. Either component recorded in isolation, or on a different date, will not satisfy the indicator. Build both into a single review template/workflow and code everything on the same day.
💡
Based on NICE IND191 COPD010 is based on NICE IND191. The MRC dyspnoea scale is available in the NICE guideline NG115, section 1.1 (diagnosing COPD), Table 1. The CAT (COPD Assessment Test) is required as part of the Locally Enhanced Service (LES) and must be completed and coded at every annual review alongside the MRC scale.

Annual COPD Review — COPD010

Use the (RP) LTC Review Template CEG v1.4 in EMIS. All QOF-required components must be coded on the same day as the review appointment.

⚠️
COPD010 — Code Both Components on the Same Day QOF business rules require the number of exacerbations and the MRC dyspnoea scale score to be recorded on the same date as the review. Coding either component on a different day risks the indicator not being met for that patient. Both must be built into the review template and completed in a single appointment.
🌬️
1. MRC Dyspnoea Scale (Required — QOF) Score and record the patient's current MRC dyspnoea grade (1–5) using the standard scale. Code the MRC grade on the day of the review. See the MRC Scale section below for full grade descriptors.
🗓️
2. Number of Exacerbations (Required — QOF) Record the number of exacerbations the patient has experienced since the last annual review (or in the preceding 12 months for new attendees). Code on the same day as the review — not retrospectively.
💨
3. Inhaler Technique Assessment (Best Practice) Assess inhaler technique face to face. Document whether technique is satisfactory and record any corrective advice given. Required for any patient prescribed an inhaled therapy — NICE NG115 requirement.
📊
4. CAT Score (Required — LES) The COPD Assessment Test (CAT) is required as part of the Locally Enhanced Service (LES). Complete and code the CAT score at every annual review. It provides a validated measure of current health status and complements the MRC scale.
📋  Additional Review Components (Best Practice, NICE NG115)
Smoking Status & Cessation

The single most effective intervention in COPD. Record smoking status at every review. Refer to the Stop Smoking service for all current smokers — document referral or patient's decision to decline.

Vaccination Status

Annual flu vaccination is the most effective clinical intervention in COPD. Check and offer/arrange flu and pneumococcal vaccinations at every review. Code vaccination or declined status.

Pulmonary Rehabilitation — LES Required (MRC ≥3)

Pulmonary rehabilitation referral for patients with MRC grade ≥3 is required as part of the LES. PR improves quality of life and reduces exacerbation frequency. Document referral or, if the patient declines, record their informed refusal.

💡
Exacerbation Frequency — Management Implications Recording the number of exacerbations is not just a QOF requirement — it directly informs personalised management. Patients with 2 or more exacerbations per year should be considered for stepped-up inhaled therapy and pulmonary rehabilitation referral. Patients with frequent or severe exacerbations should be considered for secondary care referral by the GP or Pharmacist.
4

MRC Dyspnoea Scale

The MRC dyspnoea scale gives a validated measure of breathlessness and is recommended as part of the COPD annual review (NICE NG115, section 1.1, Table 1). Record and code the grade at every review — it tracks disease progression and informs management decisions including pulmonary rehabilitation referral.

Grade Description Clinical Implication
1 Not troubled by breathlessness except on strenuous exercise Mild — optimise risk factor management; smoking cessation; annual flu vaccine
2 Short of breath when hurrying on level ground or walking up a slight hill Mild-moderate — review inhaler therapy; smoking cessation; consider PR if symptomatic
3 Walks slower than most people of the same age on level ground, or stops after about 15 minutes walking at own pace Moderate — offer pulmonary rehabilitation referral; review inhaled therapy; escalate if not improving
4 Stops for breath after walking about 100 yards, or after a few minutes on level ground Severe — PR referral; refer to secondary care (GP or Pharmacist); review full inhaled therapy; ensure rescue pack in place
5 Too breathless to leave the house, or breathless when dressing or undressing Very severe — refer to secondary/tertiary care (GP or Pharmacist); palliative care consideration; urgent rescue plan
📋
MRC Grade ≥3 — Pulmonary Rehabilitation Referral Required (LES) Pulmonary rehabilitation referral is required as part of the LES for any patient with an MRC grade of 3 or above. Document the referral or the patient's informed refusal. MRC grade 4 or 5, new or worsening breathlessness, or concern about diagnostic accuracy warrants secondary care referral — this can be made by the GP or Pharmacist.

Step-by-Step Process

1
Register Management

The GP Lead reviews the COPD register at a minimum annually and opportunistically throughout the year. An accurate register is the foundation of all QOF attainment.

  • Add patients with confirmed new COPD diagnoses (spirometry-confirmed airflow obstruction)
  • Remove patients where COPD diagnosis has been excluded or was incorrectly coded
  • Review patients with diagnostic uncertainty — ensure spirometry has been performed and is valid
  • Identify patients recently discharged after COPD admission — ensure coded and due a review
2
Identify Patients Due a Review

Admin identifies and recalls patients due a review using the QOF dashboard. The monthly meeting is used to review progress, problem-solve barriers, and manage complex cases such as persistent non-attenders.

  • Identify patients with no COPD review in the past 12 months
  • Identify patients with reviews where either MRC score or exacerbation count is missing (incomplete reviews do not satisfy COPD010)
  • Prioritise patients known to be high-risk: frequent exacerbators, MRC ≥3, recent hospital admission
3
Invite & Book
  • Admin sends personalised invitation letter or SMS to patients identified as due a review
  • Book annual COPD review with Respiratory Nurse/HCA — advise patient to bring inhalers
  • Notify patient that the appointment will include a breathing questionnaire and an inhaler technique check
  • Book follow-up Pharmacist appointment if medication review is required
  • No response after 2 weeks: send second invitation. After a further 2 weeks: telephone outreach.
  • Still no response: add to monthly QOF meeting agenda for team review
4
Conduct the Annual Review

Respiratory Nurse or HCA conducts the review using (RP) LTC Review Template CEG v1.4. Both QOF-required components (MRC grade and exacerbation count) must be coded on the same day.

  • Record MRC dyspnoea scale grade — code on the day of the review
  • Record number of exacerbations since last review — code on the same day
  • Complete CAT (COPD Assessment Test) — required for LES; code score on the day
  • Assess inhaler technique face to face with patient's own inhaler(s) — document and code
  • Check smoking status — refer to Stop Smoking service for all current smokers
  • Check and offer flu and pneumococcal vaccinations — code vaccinations or declined status
  • If MRC grade ≥3: refer to pulmonary rehabilitation — required for LES; document referral or patient's informed refusal
  • Escalate clinical concerns (MRC 4–5, frequent exacerbations, diagnostic uncertainty) to GP or Pharmacist for secondary care referral on the day
5
Medication Review (Pharmacist)

Arranged for patients with medication-related concerns identified at the annual review, or where a structured inhaled therapy review is required.

  • Review current inhaler regimen against NICE NG115 pharmacological pathways
  • Assess inhaler device for patient preference, technique, and environmental impact (low-carbon options)
  • Review appropriateness of ICS — consider stepping down if not indicated (low exacerbation rate, no asthmatic features)
  • Identify patients who may benefit from stepped-up therapy (e.g. two or more exacerbations per year)
  • Refers patients to secondary/tertiary care where criteria are met, or escalates to GP same day where GP input is required
6
Did Not Attend (DNA) Management
  • First DNA: re-invite by letter or SMS within 5 working days; note attempt in EMIS
  • Second DNA: telephone contact attempt; document outcome
  • Third DNA / no response: add to monthly QOF meeting agenda for team review
  • GP reviews repeated non-attenders; considers clinical vulnerability and appropriate next steps
  • Where exception reporting is appropriate: GP approves PCA code with documented justification
6

Exception Reporting

Patient Consent Adjustments (PCAs) allow practices to exclude individual patients from QOF indicators where it would be clinically inappropriate or impossible to include them. All exception reporting must be approved by the GP and documented with a clear clinical or administrative reason.

🩺  Clinical Exceptions
  • Patient unable to complete annual review due to severe cognitive impairment or terminal illness
  • Patient in an extended care facility where review by practice is not feasible
  • Clinical contraindication to components of the review (documented by GP)
  • Recently joined the register — patient registered within 3 months
  • New diagnosis made within 3 months of the QOF year end
📋  Administrative Exceptions
  • Patient has been invited at least twice (letter/SMS + telephone) and has not responded
  • Patient has actively declined the review — documented informed refusal
  • Patient temporarily absent from the area for the full 12-month review window
🚫
Exception reporting must never be used to improve QOF scores without clinical justification. Commissioners may audit a random sample of exceptions — incorrect coding can result in financial claw-back. Every PCA code must be approved by the GP with a documented clinical or administrative reason in the patient's record.
