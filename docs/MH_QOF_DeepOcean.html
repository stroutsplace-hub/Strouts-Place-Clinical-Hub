Standard Operating Procedure

Mental Health
QOF 2025/26

Achieving targets for MH002, MH003, MH006, MH007, MH011 & MH012 — a guide for administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
A Major Health Inequality Patients with severe mental illness (SMI) have a life expectancy 15–20 years shorter than the general population, largely due to preventable physical illness. Our QOF work directly saves lives — it is not just about points.
ℹ️
Scope All indicators apply to patients on the SMI register: schizophrenia, bipolar affective disorder, and other psychoses. This SOP covers the full patient pathway — from register identification through to clinical review, coding, and QOF monitoring.
1

QOF Indicators at a Glance

Code Indicator Points Target
MH002 Comprehensive care plan documented & agreed with patient/carer 5 40–90%
MH003 Blood pressure recorded in preceding 12 months 3 50–90%
MH006 BMI recorded in preceding 12 months 3 50–90%
MH007 Alcohol consumption recorded in preceding 12 months 3 50–90%
MH011 Lipid profile recorded (12 months high-risk / 24 months all others) 7 50–90%
MH012 Blood glucose or HbA1c recorded in preceding 12 months 7 50–90%
💡
MH011 — Who is high risk? (12-month window) Patients currently prescribed antipsychotics, AND/OR pre-existing CVD conditions (CHD, diabetes, stroke, PAD, CKD), AND/OR a current smoker, AND/OR BMI ≥23 (or ≥25 if White ethnicity recorded). All other patients: 24-month window.
💡
MH012 exclusion Patients with a confirmed diabetes diagnosis are automatically excluded — they are captured in the diabetes QOF domain.

Roles & Responsibilities

🗂️
Practice Manager
  • Owns overall QOF performance
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting to review dashboard and agree action plans
  • Coordinates recall campaign with admin lead
  • Escalates persistent non-responders to GP lead
📋
Admin / Reception Team
  • Generates SMI register reports monthly
  • Sends invitation letters and SMS recall messages
  • Books One-Stop Clinic with HCA (30 minutes)
  • Books follow-up TC with Pharmacist for care plan
  • Scans and codes results received from secondary care
🩺
Healthcare Assistant (HCA)
  • Runs the One-Stop Clinic: all physical checks and bloods in one visit
  • Documents all readings with correct SNOMED codes same day using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template
  • Completes AUDIT-C alcohol screening and codes result
💊
Pharmacist
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting with GP lead, PM and admin lead
  • Conducts TC appointment to complete care plan (MH002)
  • Reviews results and actions abnormals as per lab workflow policy
  • Completes antipsychotic physical health check and medication review as part of the Locally Enhanced Service (LES) — carried out during the follow-up TC appointment once One-Stop Clinic results are available
💊
LES — Antipsychotic Review (at follow-up TC) The pharmacist reviews the following at the follow-up TC appointment as part of the LES requirement:
  • ✓HbA1c (annual)
  • ✓HDL cholesterol (annual)
  • ✓Blood pressure (annual)
  • ✓BMI (annual)
  • ✓Medication review — antipsychotic efficacy, side effects, adherence and optimisation
  • ✓Ensure all LES components are coded using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template
ECG: Not required routinely. Only indicated if a patient's cardiac risk changes (e.g. new IHD or arrhythmia). ELFT are responsible for the initial ECG prior to antipsychotic initiation — this is not a primary care responsibility.
👨‍⚕️
GP
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting with pharmacist, PM and admin lead
  • Actions abnormal results as per lab workflow policy
  • Approves clinical exception reporting
  • Reviews patients with repeated DNAs

Step-by-Step Process

1
Identify Patients Due a Review
  • Run SMI register report monthly — patients with schizophrenia, bipolar or other psychoses diagnosis codes
  • Filter for any missing or overdue QOF indicators (MH002–MH012)
  • Prioritise patients with the most overdue items first
  • For MH011: determine high-risk status to confirm whether the 12- or 24-month window applies
2
Invite & Book
  • Send a personalised invitation letter or SMS within 5 working days of the report being run
  • Book a One-Stop Clinic appointment with HCA — 30 minutes
  • Advise patient to fast if possible (required for accurate lipid / glucose results)
  • Book a follow-up telephone appointment with the Pharmacist to review results and complete the care plan (MH002) — schedule this for after blood results are expected back from the lab
  • If no response after 2 weeks: send a second invitation. After a further 2 weeks: telephone outreach.
  • If still no response after 3 attempts: add to the monthly QOF meeting agenda for review by the team
3
One-Stop Clinic — HCA (30 minutes)

The HCA completes all physical checks and bloods in a single appointment, documented using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template. Everything must be coded on the same day.

🩺🧪  All checks & bloods — single visit
  • ✓Blood pressure (MH003)
  • ✓Lipid profile (MH011)
  • ✓Weight & BMI (MH006)
  • ✓Blood glucose / HbA1c (MH012)*
  • ✓Waist circumference if BMI ≥25
  • ✓All results coded same day in EMIS
  • ✓AUDIT-C alcohol screen (MH007)

* Exclude if patient has a confirmed diabetes diagnosis

4
Telephone Appointment — Pharmacist

Once the One-Stop Clinic is complete and blood results have been returned by the lab, the Pharmacist conducts a follow-up telephone consultation to:

  • Discuss results with the patient
  • Review, update and agree the care plan with patient and/or carer (MH002)
  • Ensure care plan is dated after any recent relapse — pre-relapse plans are not acceptable for QOF
  • Action any abnormal results as per the lab workflow policy
  • Signpost to weight management, smoking cessation or alcohol services as appropriate
⚠️
Care plan (MH002) requirements — Must be: documented in the last 12 months, agreed with the patient and/or carer, updated following any relapse. If managed in community mental health services, a documented CMHS care plan is acceptable provided the practice holds evidence of a review.
5
Coding & Documentation
  • All results coded in the patient record on the day of the appointment
  • Use correct SNOMED/Read codes as required by EMIS
  • Blood results received from secondary care: scan and code on receipt
  • If the patient is on antipsychotics, verify lipid profile window (12 months) is applied correctly
6
Exception Reporting

Exceptions may be entered by any appropriate member of staff but must be approved by the GP, and only where clinically justified.

  • Patient has been informed of the recommendation but declines (patient declined)
  • Clinical contraindication documented and approved by the GP
  • Patient recently registered (within 3 months) — new patient exception
⚠️
Exception reporting must never be used to improve QOF scores without clinical justification. Commissioners may audit a random sample of exceptions — incorrect coding can result in financial claw-back.
4

Monitoring & QOF Meeting

📅
Monthly QOF Meeting The GP Lead, Pharmacist, Practice Manager and Admin Lead meet monthly to review the QOF dashboard, assess performance against targets, problem-solve barriers and agree actionable improvement plans for the following month.
Frequency Activity Owner
Monthly Run SMI register exception report; review outstanding patients Practice Manager / Admin Lead
Monthly QOF meeting — GP Lead, Pharmacist, PM and Admin Lead review dashboard, performance and agree action plans GP Lead (Chair)
Monthly Check QOF dashboard for current achievement against thresholds Practice Manager
Annually Full register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, remove incorrect entries GP Lead
As needed Patients with 3+ failed contacts or DNAs raised at monthly QOF meeting for team review Admin Lead → QOF Meeting
