Standard Operating Procedure

Cholesterol &
Lipid Management 2025/26

Achieving targets for CHOL003 & CHOL004 — a guide for administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
A Leading Preventable Cause of Death High cholesterol is one of the most significant risk factors for cardiovascular disease. Globally, a third of ischaemic heart disease is attributable to high cholesterol. In England, it is estimated to account for 7.1% of deaths and 3.7% of disability-adjusted life years. For patients with established cardiovascular disease, effective lipid-lowering treatment significantly reduces the risk of further events, hospitalisation and death.
ℹ️
Scope CHOL003 applies to patients on the Coronary Heart Disease (CHD), Peripheral Arterial Disease (PAD), Stroke/TIA or Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) register. CHOL004 applies to patients on the CHD, PAD or Stroke/TIA register only. This SOP covers the full review pathway — from register identification through to lipid measurement, therapy optimisation, coding, and QOF monitoring.

⚠️
CHOL004 — The most recent result counts QOF uses the most recent cholesterol result in the preceding 12 months. Where multiple readings exist on the same most recent date, LDL takes priority over non-HDL. Ensuring timely repeat testing after therapy intensification is critical — results recorded before year-end (31 March) determine attainment.
💡
CHOL003 — What counts as a success? A high-intensity statin is first-line therapy. Where this is declined or clinically unsuitable due to contraindication or intolerance, the following alternatives are included as a 'success' for CHOL003: ezetimibe (± bempedoic acid if insufficient response), PCSK9 inhibitors (alirocumab or evolocumab, for LDL persistently >3.5 or 4.0 mmol/L depending on risk profile), and inclisiran (for LDL persistently ≥2.6 mmol/L). The reason for not prescribing a statin must be clearly documented.
💊  Lipid-Lowering Therapy — Treatment Pathway
1st Line — High-Intensity Statin

Recommended for all patients with established CVD for secondary prevention. Options: atorvastatin 40–80 mg or rosuvastatin 20–40 mg daily.

2nd Line — If Statin Declined / Not Tolerated

Ezetimibe monotherapy. If insufficient reduction: add bempedoic acid (NICE TA694 — statin intolerance pathway). Document reason for statin absence clearly.

3rd Line — Injectable Therapies

PCSK9 inhibitors (alirocumab / evolocumab) if LDL >3.5–4.0 mmol/L. Inclisiran if LDL ≥2.6 mmol/L. Refer to specialist lipid service or initiate via agreed local pathway.

Roles & Responsibilities

🗂️
Practice Manager
  • Owns overall QOF performance
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting; reviews CHOL003 and CHOL004 dashboard performance
  • Coordinates recall campaign with admin team following each QOF meeting
  • Chairs escalation of persistent non-responders at the monthly QOF meeting
📋
Admin / Reception Team
  • Sends invitation letters and SMS to patients due for lipid review when directed following the monthly QOF meeting
  • Books blood test appointments with HCA or phlebotomy
  • Books follow-up Pharmacist appointment once bloods are available
  • Codes results received from secondary care on receipt
  • Logs DNA attempts and adds non-responders to the monthly QOF meeting agenda
🩺
Healthcare Assistant (HCA)
  • Requests fasting lipid profile (or non-fasting if patient unable to fast) using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template in EMIS
  • Records weight, BMI, smoking status and alcohol (AUDIT-C)
  • Ensures blood test results are linked to the correct patient record on receipt
  • Codes all findings on the day of the appointment
  • Flags results that appear abnormal or out of expected range to the Pharmacist or GP
💊
Pharmacist
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting with GP Lead, PM and Admin Lead
  • Reviews lipid results at medication review appointment (TC or F2F)
  • Confirms statin is prescribed and coded (CHOL003); intensifies therapy where CHOL004 target not achieved
  • Manages statin intolerance pathway: switches to ezetimibe ± bempedoic acid as appropriate
  • Refers to specialist lipid service or initiates injectable therapy via agreed pathway where LDL persistently elevated despite oral therapy
  • Arranges repeat lipid monitoring after any therapy change (ideally within 8–12 weeks)
  • Escalates to GP if clinical concerns are identified
👨‍⚕️
GP Lead
  • Chairs monthly QOF meeting; approves action plan
  • Reviews and approves all exception reports
  • Reviews patients escalated by the Pharmacist with complex lipid management needs
  • Manages annual register accuracy review — ensures CHD, PAD, Stroke/TIA and CKD registers are correct and coding is up to date
  • Reviews patients declining treatment — documents shared decision-making in the notes

Step-by-Step Process

1
Register Identification & Recall

At the monthly QOF meeting, the team reviews the CHOL003 and CHOL004 dashboards and identifies patients requiring recall. The Practice Manager informs admin of patients to invite.

  • Pull patients due for annual lipid review from CHOL003 and CHOL004 QOF reports
  • Identify patients without a lipid result in the preceding 12 months (CHOL004)
  • Identify patients not currently prescribed a statin or alternative (CHOL003)
  • Send invitation letter or SMS — book a blood test with HCA or phlebotomy
  • If no response after 2 weeks: send a second invitation. After a further 2 weeks: telephone outreach
  • If still no response: add to the monthly QOF meeting agenda for team review
2
Blood Test — Lipid Profile

The HCA or phlebotomist requests a fasting lipid profile. All results must be filed and coded in EMIS on the same day they are received.

🧪  Lipid profile — what to request
  • ✓Total cholesterol
  • ✓LDL cholesterol (direct)
  • ✓HDL cholesterol
  • ✓Non-HDL cholesterol (calculated)
  • ✓Triglycerides
  • ✓Liver function tests (if on statin — annual monitoring)
⚠️
QOF reading priority — LDL takes precedence Where both an LDL and a non-HDL reading are available on the same most recent date, QOF uses the LDL reading to assess CHOL004 attainment. Ensure the lab request generates both values wherever possible.
3
Medication Review & Therapy Optimisation — Pharmacist

Once lipid results are available, admin books a medication review TC or F2F with the Pharmacist. The Pharmacist reviews results, confirms CHOL003 status and acts on CHOL004 attainment.

  • CHOL003: Confirm the patient is currently prescribed a statin or documented alternative. If no lipid-lowering therapy is prescribed and no exception recorded, initiate or escalate to GP.
  • CHOL004 — Target achieved (LDL ≤2.0 or non-HDL ≤2.6 mmol/L): Document result and continue current therapy. Code the cholesterol reading in EMIS on the date of the result.
  • CHOL004 — Target not achieved: Review therapy — consider up-titrating statin dose, adding ezetimibe, or referring for injectable therapy. Arrange repeat lipid profile in 8–12 weeks.
  • Review statin adherence — address barriers, offer tablet splitting or alternative dosing strategies where appropriate
  • Assess for statin side effects — myalgia, hepatotoxicity (check LFTs). If confirmed intolerance, document clearly and initiate the statin intolerance pathway
  • Signpost to lifestyle interventions: diet (Mediterranean / low-saturated-fat), physical activity, smoking cessation, alcohol reduction
  • Escalate to GP if clinical concerns or complex lipid phenotype (consider familial hypercholesterolaemia)
🔄
Repeat testing after therapy intensification After any change in lipid-lowering therapy, arrange a repeat fasting lipid profile in 8–12 weeks. This result will become the most recent reading in QOF and should ideally be available before 31 March. Allow sufficient time for the treatment cycle to complete — do not intensify therapy in late February or March without booking the follow-up test.
🔬  Statin Intolerance Pathway (Pharmacist)
Document first
  • ·Nature of side effect (myalgia, LFT elevation, other)
  • ·Statin(s) tried and doses
  • ·Patient agreement to alternative therapy
Then prescribe
  • ·Ezetimibe 10 mg daily (first choice)
  • ·Add bempedoic acid if ezetimibe insufficient (NICE TA694)
  • ·Consider PCSK9i / inclisiran if LDL still persistently elevated

Statin intolerance must be clearly coded in EMIS with the appropriate exception or alternative therapy to count for CHOL003. Escalate to GP if uncertain.

4
Coding & Documentation
  • All lipid results coded in EMIS on the date the result is received — use the correct SNOMED codes for LDL, HDL, non-HDL and total cholesterol
  • CHOL003: statin or alternative lipid-lowering therapy must be on the active medication list — check it has not been stopped or marked as not taken
  • Where a statin is not prescribed: code the reason — 'patient declined', 'contraindicated', or 'intolerance' as appropriate
  • Use the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template to document the full review
  • Secondary care correspondence: admin to scan and code relevant results and medication changes on receipt — particularly lipid results from hospital, specialist clinics or cardiology
5
Exception Reporting

Exceptions may be entered by any appropriate member of staff but must be approved by the GP, and only where clinically justified.

  • Patient has been informed of the recommendation but declines (patient declined)
  • Clinical contraindication documented and approved by the GP (e.g. active liver disease, drug interaction)
  • Patient recently registered (within 3 months) — new patient exception
  • Maximum tolerated lipid-lowering therapy already in use and target not achievable
⚠️
Exception reporting must never be used to improve QOF scores without clinical justification. Commissioners may audit a random sample of exceptions — incorrect coding can result in financial claw-back.
4

Monitoring & QOF Meeting

📅
Monthly QOF Meeting The GP Lead, Pharmacist, Practice Manager and Admin Lead meet monthly to review the QOF dashboard, assess performance against CHOL003 and CHOL004, problem-solve barriers and agree an actionable improvement plan. This meeting is also the forum for reviewing patients who have not responded to recall — the team agrees the approach collectively.
Frequency Activity Owner
Monthly QOF meeting — GP Lead, Pharmacist, PM and Admin Lead review CHOL003 / CHOL004 dashboard, identify patients to recall, review non-responders and agree action plan GP Lead (Chair)
Monthly Admin team informed of patients to invite for blood test and Pharmacist review following QOF meeting Practice Manager / Admin Lead
Monthly Pharmacist reviews patients with lipid results not at CHOL004 target — confirms repeat test and therapy change are booked Pharmacist
As needed Patients with DNA or declining attendance reviewed at monthly QOF meeting — team agrees approach; GP involved only if clinical concerns QOF Meeting
Annually Full register accuracy review — add new diagnoses to CHD, PAD, Stroke/TIA and CKD registers; remove incorrect entries; validate coding GP Lead
Annually Review patients on alternative lipid-lowering therapy (non-statin) — confirm indication, tolerability and ongoing appropriateness Pharmacist / GP Lead
