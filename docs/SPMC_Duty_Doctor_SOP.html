Overview

Overview of the Role

Purpose and scope

The Duty Doctor is responsible for managing all same-day and urgent clinical matters that arise during their duty session. This includes triage, reviewing urgent clinical information, and providing timely clinical support to the wider team.

This SOP applies to all GPs undertaking the Duty Doctor role within the practice.

Klinik Triage

Online request management

2.1  Disclaimer

The GP surgery is not an emergency service. Despite this, we will always do our best to support and assist our patients in whatever situation they may be in.

"This service is only available to patients registered with Strouts Place Medical Centre. Our opening hours are 8 a.m. to 6:30 p.m., Monday to Friday. You can contact us online anytime during our opening hours. If you have already been invited for an appointment, either via phone or letter, you can call the Practice to book this and do not need to raise an online request. Please note after 4:30 p.m. your query may not be reviewed until the next working day. The online consultation tool should be used for routine queries only after this time. If you have any urgent queries after this time, it is important you contact the surgery directly to avoid any delays in care. In a life threatening emergency, contact 999."
Duty Doctors should be mindful of this message when reviewing Klinik submissions after 4:30 p.m. Routine queries submitted after this time may not be reviewed until the next working day. Patients with urgent queries after 4:30 p.m. should be directed to contact the surgery directly. In a life-threatening emergency, patients should call 999.

2.4  Duty Doctor Responsibilities

  • Manage Klinik triage throughout the session, reviewing all patient submissions promptly.
  • Prioritise cases according to clinical urgency.
  • Allocate cases appropriately to other clinicians, allied health professionals, or admin staff when suitable.
  • Contact patients directly for further assessment or advice as required.
Clinical Responsibilities
3

Clinician Queries

Supporting the wider clinical team
  • Provide clinical support to other members of the team, including nurses, HCAs, pharmacists, and administrative staff.
  • Offer advice on prescribing, patient management, and urgent care queries.
  • Review and sign off tasks referred by other clinicians where appropriate.
4

Review of Lab Reports

Results management and delegation
  • Regularly review and action lab reports.
  • Ensure urgent or abnormal results are communicated to patients or managed appropriately.
  • Allocate appropriate lab results to other clinicians.

5

Urgent Documents

Hospital letters, discharge summaries, urgent correspondence
  • Review and action urgent clinical documents, including hospital letters, discharge summaries, and correspondence marked as urgent.
  • Ensure necessary follow-up actions are documented and communicated clearly.
6

Urgent Queries

Tasks, phone calls, and internal messages
  • Respond promptly to urgent administrative or clinical queries received via tasks, phone calls, or internal messages.
  • Liaise with reception and admin staff to ensure timely patient communication and safety netting.
7

Medicines Management

Prescriptions and medication queries
  • Review and approve prescription requests where clinically appropriate.
  • Address medication queries from pharmacies or patients that require same-day action.
  • Support safe prescribing practices within the team.

8

Communication & Escalation

Workload management and handover
  • Maintain clear communication with the reception and admin teams regarding workload and prioritisation.
  • Escalate any significant concerns or safety issues to the leadership team (managers or partners).
  • Ensure handover of any ongoing urgent matters at the end of the session.
9

Documentation

Clinical record keeping
  • Record all patient interactions, decisions, and actions clearly in the clinical record.
  • Ensure tasks are completed or delegated appropriately with clear documentation.

Review

This SOP will be reviewed annually, or earlier if there are changes to practice workflow, clinical systems, or patient access arrangements.
