Standard Operating Procedure

Hypertension
QOF Management 2025/26

Achieving targets for HYP008 & HYP009 — a guide for administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
The UK's Leading Modifiable Cardiovascular Risk Factor Hypertension affects around 1 in 3 adults in England and is the biggest single risk factor for stroke, MI, heart failure, and premature death. Many patients remain undiagnosed, untreated, or sub-optimally managed.
ℹ️
Scope Applies to all patients on the Hypertension register. Covers HYP008 (aged ≤79) and HYP009 (aged ≥80) — including recall, BP measurement, treatment escalation, exception coding, and performance monitoring.
1

QOF Indicators at a Glance

Code Indicator Points Target
HYP008 Patients aged ≤79 years with hypertension in whom the last BP reading (preceding 12 months) is ≤140/90 mmHg (clinic) or ≤135/85 mmHg (HBPM) 38 40–85%
HYP009 Patients aged ≥80 years with hypertension in whom the last BP reading (preceding 12 months) is ≤150/90 mmHg (clinic) or ≤145/85 mmHg (HBPM) 14 40–85%

Roles & Responsibilities

🗂️
Practice Manager
  • Owns overall QOF performance
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting
  • Coordinates recall campaign with admin team
  • Chairs escalation of persistent non-responders
📋
Admin / Reception Team
  • Generates hypertension register reports
  • Sends invitation letters and SMS for BP review
  • Books BP check appointments with HCA, nurse or pharmacist
  • Logs DNA attempts and adds non-responders to QOF meeting agenda
🩺
Healthcare Assistant (HCA)
  • Takes and records clinic BP using a validated device and correct technique
  • Records weight, BMI, smoking and alcohol intake
  • Codes all readings on the day using correct SNOMED CT codes
  • Flags elevated readings to nurse or pharmacist for review
💊
Pharmacist
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting
  • Conducts medication reviews — adherence, side-effects, dose optimisation
  • Optimises treatment per NICE NG136 stepwise pathway
  • Runs targeted recall clinics for uncontrolled or near-miss BP
  • Escalates to GP where appropriate
👨‍⚕️
GP Lead
  • Chairs monthly QOF meeting
  • Reviews and approves exception reports
  • Manages resistant hypertension and complex clinical needs
  • Annual register accuracy review
3

Step-by-Step Process

1
Identify Patients Due a Review
  • Run hypertension register report monthly
  • Prioritise patients whose last BP was above target
  • Flag patients approaching year-end (31 March) without a qualifying reading
2
Invite & Book
  • Send invitation letter or SMS within 5 working days of report
  • Book BP review with HCA, nurse or pharmacist
  • No response after 2 weeks: second invite. After further 2 weeks: telephone outreach
  • 3 failed attempts: add to QOF meeting agenda
3
BP Review Appointment — HCA / Nurse / Pharmacist

Complete all measurements and code in EMIS on the same day.

Use the (RP) LTC Review Template CEG v1.4 in EMIS.

🩺  BP Review — what to complete
  • ✓BP (clinic) — validated device, correct technique
  • ✓Or HBPM average — coded with HBPM SNOMED code
  • ✓Weight & BMI
  • ✓Smoking status
  • ✓Alcohol status (AUDIT-C if not recent)
  • ✓QRISK3 calculated & coded
  • ✓Medication review — adherence, side-effects (pharmacist only)
  • ✓Lifestyle advice coded (diet, salt, exercise)
  • ✓All results coded same day in EMIS
4
Treatment Escalation — Pharmacist / GP

Where BP is above target, review and optimise before adding agents. Repeat BP within 4–8 weeks of any medication change.

  • Step 1: ACEi or ARB (aged <55, non-Black African/Caribbean) or CCB (aged ≥55 or Black African/Caribbean)
  • Step 2: ACEi or ARB + CCB
  • Step 3: ACEi or ARB + CCB + thiazide-like diuretic (e.g. indapamide)
  • Step 4: Spironolactone if K⁺ <4.5, or alpha-/beta-blocker if K⁺ ≥4.5 — consider specialist referral
  • Check adherence before stepping up
5
Coding & Documentation
  • Code all results on the day of appointment
  • Clinic BP: correct SNOMED BP code with systolic and diastolic values
  • HBPM: HBPM-specific SNOMED code — a generic BP code will not satisfy QOF
  • Medication changes: prescribe and link to clinical record same day
6
Exception Reporting

May be entered by any appropriate staff member but must be approved by the GP, and only where clinically justified.

  • Patient informed but declines (patient declined)
  • Clinical contraindication approved by GP (e.g. severe frailty, terminal illness)
  • Registered within 3 months — new patient exception
⚠️
Exception reporting must never be used to improve QOF scores without clinical justification. Commissioners may audit a random sample of exceptions — incorrect coding can result in financial claw-back.
4

Monitoring & QOF Meeting

📅
Monthly QOF Meeting The GP Lead, Pharmacist, Practice Manager and Admin Lead meet monthly to review the QOF dashboard, assess performance against targets, problem-solve barriers and agree actionable improvement plans for the following month.
Frequency Activity Owner
Monthly Run hypertension register report; identify patients without a qualifying BP in the preceding 12 months Admin Lead
Monthly QOF meeting — review dashboard, performance and agree action plans GP Lead (Chair)
Monthly Check HYP008 and HYP009 achievement against thresholds Practice Manager + Pharmacist
Annually Full register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, remove incorrect entries GP Lead
As needed 3+ failed contacts or DNAs raised at QOF meeting for team review Admin Lead → QOF Meeting
