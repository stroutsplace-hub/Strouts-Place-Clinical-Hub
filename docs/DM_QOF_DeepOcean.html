Standard Operating Procedure

Diabetes
QOF 2025/26

Achieving targets for DM006, DM012, DM014, DM020, DM021, DM034, DM035 & DM036 — a guide for administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
Scale & Impact In 2023/24 approximately 3.7 million people in England live with diagnosed diabetes. Effective monitoring and control in primary care directly reduces mortality and morbidity from both micro- and macrovascular complications. Much of this work is done by GPs and the wider primary care team.
ℹ️
Scope All indicators apply to patients on the diabetes register (type 1 and type 2 unless otherwise specified). This SOP covers the full annual review pathway — from register identification through clinical review, structured education referral, coding, and QOF monitoring. Frailty status is central to several indicators: ensure each patient has a current frailty assessment recorded.
1

QOF Indicators at a Glance

Code Indicator Points Target
DM006 Patients with nephropathy (proteinuria or micro-albuminuria) currently treated with ACE-I or ARB 3 57–97%
DM012 Foot examination and risk classification recorded in preceding 12 months 4 50–90%
DM014 Newly diagnosed patients referred to structured education programme within 9 months of diagnosis 11 40–90%
DM036 Patients aged ≤79, without moderate/severe frailty: last BP ≤140/90 mmHg in preceding 12 months 27 38–90%
DM020 Patients without moderate/severe frailty: last HbA1c ≤58 mmol/mol in preceding 12 months 17 35–75%
DM021 Patients with moderate/severe frailty: last HbA1c ≤75 mmol/mol in preceding 12 months 10 52–92%
DM034 Patients aged ≥40, no CVD history, without moderate/severe frailty: treated with statin (or alternative lipid-lowering therapy if statin declined/unsuitable), or CVD risk <10% recorded in preceding 3 years 4 50–90%
DM035 Patients with history of CVD (excl. haemorrhagic stroke): treated with statin or alternative lipid-lowering therapy if statin declined/unsuitable 2 50–90%
💡
DM014 — Timing detail Nine months is defined as 279 days from entry onto the diabetes register. Digital programmes (e.g. Healthy Living with Type 2 Diabetes) are acceptable and can be offered at any point post-diagnosis, supporting annual reinforcement.
💡
DM036 — Home BP monitoring The clinic target of 140/90 mmHg corresponds to an HBPM target of 135/85 mmHg. Document clearly which method was used when recording the reading.
⚠️
DM034 — QRISK3 exclusion Patients with type 2 diabetes who have a CVD risk of less than 10% recorded in the preceding 3 years are excluded from the denominator for DM034. Ensure a current QRISK3 score is documented for all eligible patients.

Roles & Responsibilities

🗂️
Practice Manager
  • Owns overall QOF performance
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting; reviews dashboard and agrees action plans
  • Coordinates annual review recall campaign with admin lead
  • Escalates persistent non-responders to GP lead
📋
Admin / Reception Team
  • Generates diabetes register reports to identify patients due a review
  • Sends invitation letters and SMS for annual review
  • Books annual review appointment with HCA or Nurse
  • Scans and codes results received from secondary care
🩺
Healthcare Assistant (HCA) / Nurse
  • Annual review: BP, HbA1c, lipid profile, urine ACR, foot exam + risk classification, BMI, smoking, alcohol status, QRISK3 calculation, retinal screening status check
  • New diabetic review: all of the above, plus structured education referral (DM014), retinal screening referral — both coded on the day
  • Documents all readings with correct SNOMED codes same day using the EMIS diabetes template
  • Flags any abnormal results to GP or pharmacist for action as per surgery policy
💊
Pharmacist
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting with GP lead, PM and admin lead
  • Conducts medication review — statin prescribing (DM034/035), ACE-I/ARB for nephropathy (DM006)
  • Reviews results and actions abnormals as per surgery policy
  • Optimises BP and diabetic control; adjusts or initiates therapy as appropriate
  • Arranges follow-up for patients requiring monitoring or treatment changes
👨‍⚕️
GP
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting; chairs review
  • Reviews and acts on abnormal results as per surgery policy
  • Manages patients requiring clinical escalation
  • Approves clinical exception reporting
  • Annual register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, remove incorrect entries

Step-by-Step Process

1
Identify Patients Due an Annual Review
  • Run diabetes register report monthly — patients with type 1 or type 2 diabetes diagnosis codes
  • Filter for overdue or missing QOF indicators (DM006, DM012, DM014, DM020/021, DM034/035, DM036)
  • For DM034: check whether a current QRISK3 score (<3 years) is documented — if so and <10%, patient is excluded from denominator
  • For newly diagnosed patients: flag those approaching the 279-day window for DM014 structured education referral
2
Invite & Book
  • Send a personalised invitation letter or SMS within 5 working days of the report being run
  • Book an annual review appointment with HCA or Nurse — allow at least 30 minutes
  • If no response after 2 weeks: send a second invitation. After a further 2 weeks: telephone outreach.
  • If still no response after 3 attempts: add to the monthly QOF meeting agenda for review
3
Review Appointment — HCA / Nurse

Complete all measurements, bloods and assessments using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template in EMIS. Everything must be coded on the same day.

🔄  Annual Review — all established patients
  • ✓Blood pressure (DM036)
  • ✓Urine ACR (for DM006 — nephropathy)
  • ✓HbA1c (DM020/DM021)
  • ✓Foot exam + risk classification (DM012)
  • ✓Lipid profile (for DM034/035 statin review)
  • ✓Weight & BMI
  • ✓Smoking status
  • ✓Alcohol status (AUDIT-C)
  • ✓Retinal screening — check status & date
  • ✓QRISK3 calculated & coded
  • ✓All results coded same day in EMIS
🆕  New Diabetic Review — additional items
  • ✓All annual review checks above
  • ✓Structured education referral (DM014) — coded same day
  • ✓Retinal screening referral — made and coded

DM014 referral must be made within 279 days of entry onto the diabetes register

⚠️
Abnormal findings — Any abnormal results identified during the review must be flagged to the pharmacist or GP for action as per surgery policy.
No / Mild Frailty (DM020 + DM036)
  • HbA1c target ≤58 mmol/mol
  • BP target ≤140/90 mmHg (clinic) or ≤135/85 mmHg (HBPM)
  • DM036 applies to patients aged ≤79 only
Moderate / Severe Frailty (DM021)
  • HbA1c target ≤75 mmol/mol
  • DM036 does not apply — no BP QOF target
  • Individualise targets — avoid overtreatment
4
Medication Review — Pharmacist or GP

Once the annual review results are available, the Pharmacist or GP reviews medications and acts on results.

  • DM006: If ACR ≥3 mg/mmol (micro-albuminuria or proteinuria) and patient is not on ACE-I or ARB — initiate or refer for initiation. Also consider SGLT2i per NICE guidance for type 2 diabetes with renal disease.
  • DM034: Confirm statin prescribing for eligible patients aged ≥40 without CVD history; calculate/verify QRISK3 if not done in past 3 years; document statin as high-intensity (atorvastatin 20 mg for primary prevention). Where statin is declined or unsuitable, prescribe alternative lipid-lowering therapy and document reason.
  • DM035: Confirm statin prescribing for all patients with a history of CVD (excluding haemorrhagic stroke); atorvastatin 80 mg for secondary prevention. Where statin is declined or unsuitable, prescribe alternative and document reason.
  • Action any other abnormal results as per the lab workflow policy
  • Signpost to weight management, smoking cessation, podiatry or other services as appropriate
⚠️
Statin prescribing (DM034/035) — High-intensity statin therapy is recommended: atorvastatin 20 mg for primary prevention; atorvastatin 80 mg for secondary prevention. Document any switch to an alternative lipid-lowering therapy with the clinical reason.
5
Structured Education Referral — DM014

All newly diagnosed patients must be referred to a structured education programme within 279 days of entry onto the diabetes register.

  • Make referral at the time of diagnosis or as soon as practicable — do not delay to the annual review
  • Code the referral on the day it is made; this is what counts for QOF — completion of the programme is not required
  • If patient is unable or unwilling to attend group education, offer an equivalent individual or digital alternative and document this
  • Admin team: run a monthly check of newly diagnosed patients approaching the 279-day window
6
Coding & Documentation
  • All results coded in the patient record on the day of the appointment using the EMIS diabetes template
  • Foot examination: code the specific risk classification level — low, increased (moderate), high, or ulcerated. The term 'moderate risk' is used in clinical coding for the QOF concept of 'increased risk'.
  • BP readings: document whether clinic or home measurement and record the method used
  • Blood results from secondary care: scan and code on receipt — do not wait for the annual review
  • Frailty: ensure a current eFI / frailty score is recorded and coded in the patient record
7
Exception Reporting

Exceptions may be entered by any appropriate member of staff but must be approved by the GP, and only where clinically justified.

  • Patient has been informed of the recommendation but declines (patient declined)
  • Clinical contraindication documented and approved by the GP
  • Patient recently registered (within 3 months) — new patient exception
⚠️
Exception reporting must never be used to improve QOF scores without clinical justification. Commissioners may audit a random sample of exceptions — incorrect coding can result in financial claw-back.

Monitoring & QOF Meeting

📅
Monthly QOF Meeting The GP Lead, Pharmacist, Practice Manager and Admin Lead meet monthly to review the QOF dashboard, assess performance against targets, problem-solve barriers and agree actionable improvement plans for the following month.
Frequency Activity Owner
Monthly Run diabetes register report; identify patients with overdue indicators or approaching 279-day DM014 window Practice Manager / Admin Lead
Monthly QOF meeting — GP Lead, Pharmacist, PM and Admin Lead review dashboard, performance and agree action plans GP Lead (Chair)
Monthly Check QOF dashboard for current achievement against thresholds; review statin and ACE-I/ARB prescribing gaps Practice Manager + Pharmacist
Annually Full register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, remove incorrect entries, update frailty scores GP Lead
Monthly QOF meeting agenda to include patients with 3+ failed contacts — discuss and agree approach as a team Admin Lead + QOF Meeting
