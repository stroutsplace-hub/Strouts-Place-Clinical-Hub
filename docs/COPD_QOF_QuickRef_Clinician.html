📋 QOF Indicator at a Glance
Code Indicator Points Target Window
COPD010 Annual review including: number of exacerbations recorded and MRC dyspnoea scale assessed — both coded on the same day as the review 9 pts 50–90% Preceding 12 months
⚠️
Both COPD010 components must be coded on the same day as the review. Either component recorded on a different date means the indicator is not met for that patient.
📝 Annual Review Components

Use (RP) LTC Review Template CEG v1.4 in EMIS.

🌬️ 1. MRC Dyspnoea Scale QOF
  • Score and record MRC grade 1–5 with the patient
  • Code on the same day as the review
  • See MRC scale below for grade descriptors and actions
🗓️ 2. Number of Exacerbations QOF
  • Record number of exacerbations since the last review
  • Include OCS courses and unscheduled attendances
  • Code on the same day as the review
📊 3. CAT Score LES Required
  • Complete the COPD Assessment Test (CAT) with the patient
  • Score out of 40 — code result on the day of review
  • See CAT scoring guide below
🏃 4. Pulmonary Rehabilitation LES Required (MRC ≥3)
  • Refer if MRC grade ≥3 — required for LES
  • Document referral or patient's informed refusal
  • PR improves quality of life and reduces exacerbations
💨 5. Inhaler Technique
  • Assess face to face with patient's own inhaler(s)
  • Document whether satisfactory; record corrective advice given
🚬 6. Smoking Status
  • Record smoking status at every review
  • Refer all current smokers to Stop Smoking service
  • Document referral or informed refusal
💉 7. Vaccinations
  • Check flu and pneumococcal vaccination status
  • Offer/arrange if due — annual flu vaccine is the most impactful COPD intervention
  • Code vaccinated or declined
🌬️ MRC Dyspnoea Scale — Grade Descriptors & Actions
Grade Description Action
1 Not troubled by breathlessness except on strenuous exercise Optimise risk factors; smoking cessation; annual flu vaccine
2 Short of breath when hurrying on level ground or up a slight hill Review inhaler therapy; smoking cessation; consider PR if symptomatic
3 Walks slower than contemporaries on level ground or stops after ~15 minutes at own pace Refer to pulmonary rehabilitation (LES required); review inhaled therapy
4 Stops for breath after ~100 yards or after a few minutes on level ground PR referral; refer to secondary care (GP or Pharmacist); review full inhaled therapy; ensure rescue pack in place
5 Too breathless to leave the house, or breathless when dressing/undressing Refer to secondary/tertiary care (GP or Pharmacist); palliative care consideration; urgent rescue plan
📊 CAT Score — Interpretation Guide LES Required

8 questions, each scored 0–5. Maximum score = 40. Higher score = greater impact on health status.

0–9
Low Impact

COPD has little effect on daily life. Maintain current management; reinforce smoking cessation and vaccinations.

10–20
Medium Impact

Review inhaler technique and adherence. Consider stepped-up therapy. PR if MRC ≥3.

21–30
High Impact

Review therapy and adherence. PR referral. Consider secondary care referral if not improving.

31–40
Very High Impact

Significant burden. Secondary care referral (GP or Pharmacist). Urgent review of full management plan.

⚠️
Before stepping up treatment always check adherence via prescription records and reassess inhaler technique — poor adherence and technique are common causes of high CAT scores.
⚡ Exacerbation Frequency — Management Implications
⚠️ 2+ Exacerbations per Year
  • Consider stepped-up inhaled therapy — review with Pharmacist
  • Offer pulmonary rehabilitation referral (also LES required if MRC ≥3)
  • Ensure rescue pack (prednisolone + antibiotic) is in place
  • Review inhaler technique and adherence
🚨 Frequent or Severe Exacerbations
  • Consider secondary care referral — GP or Pharmacist can refer
  • Review for ICS appropriateness if frequent exacerbator
  • Check for comorbidities contributing to exacerbation frequency
  • Ensure self-management plan is up to date
💊 Inhaled Therapy — Key Principles (NICE NG115)
🔵 SABA (Reliever)
  • For symptom relief — use as needed
  • Frequent SABA use indicates poor control — review
  • Not sufficient as sole long-term therapy
🟢 LABA / LAMA (Maintenance)
  • First-line maintenance therapy for breathlessness
  • LAMA preferred if available and tolerated
  • Consider dual LABA/LAMA for inadequate symptom control
⚠️ ICS — Use with Caution
  • Add ICS only where ≥2 exacerbations/year or asthmatic features
  • Risk of pneumonia — do not add without documented indication
  • Consider stepping down ICS if no ongoing indication
🌿
Consider environmental impact when reviewing inhalers — offer low-carbon device options where clinically appropriate and acceptable to the patient. Document device choice discussion.
🔁 Referral to Secondary Care — GP or Pharmacist Can Refer
🚨 Refer to Secondary Care
  • MRC grade 4 or 5
  • Frequent or severe exacerbations despite optimised therapy
  • Diagnostic uncertainty — spirometry not confirming obstruction
  • Suspected alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency
  • Rapid decline in FEV1 or significant weight loss
  • CAT score 31–40 not responding to primary care management
✅ Before Referring — Check
  • Inhaler technique assessed and corrected
  • Adherence confirmed via prescription records
  • Smoking cessation offered and documented
  • Pulmonary rehabilitation offered (if MRC ≥3)
  • Comorbidities (HF, anaemia, anxiety) considered and managed
