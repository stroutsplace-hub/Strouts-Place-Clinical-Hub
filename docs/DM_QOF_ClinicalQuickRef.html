🧓 Frailty-Adjusted Targets
No / Mild Frailty
  • HbA1c: ≤58 mmol/mol (DM020)
  • BP (clinic): ≤140/90 mmHg (DM036)
  • BP (HBPM): ≤135/85 mmHg (DM036)
  • DM036 applies to patients aged ≤79 only
  • DM034 statin indicator applies
Moderate / Severe Frailty
  • HbA1c: ≤75 mmol/mol (DM021)
  • BP: No QOF target — DM036 excluded
  • DM034 statin indicator excluded
  • Individualise targets — avoid overtreatment
  • Ensure frailty coded (eFI) in patient record

🫘 Nephropathy — DM006
When to act
  • ACR ≥3 mg/mmol (micro-albuminuria or proteinuria)
  • Patient not currently on ACE-I or ARB → initiate
  • Aim for maximum tolerated dose (per BNF)
Also consider
  • SGLT2 inhibitor for type 2 diabetes with renal disease — offer or consider depending on ACR level (per NICE NG28)
  • Review BP control alongside nephropathy management
🦶 Foot Risk Classification — DM012
Low Risk
Normal sensation, palpable pulses
Increased Risk
Neuropathy or absent pulses
Code as 'moderate risk' in EMIS
High Risk
Neuropathy or absent pulses plus deformity, skin changes or previous ulcer
Ulcerated
Active foot ulcer present
⚠️
'Increased risk' is coded as 'moderate risk' in EMIS — this is correct for QOF purposes.

⚡ At-a-Glance Decision Aid
Patient has ACR ≥3 mg/mmol?
  • Not on ACE-I/ARB → start one
  • T2DM + renal disease → consider SGLT2i
  • Codes for DM006 ✓
HbA1c above target?
  • No/mild frailty: target ≤58 (DM020) → intensify treatment
  • Mod/severe frailty: target ≤75 (DM021) → avoid overtreatment
  • Pharmacist to optimise and arrange follow-up
BP above target?
  • No/mild frailty, age ≤79: target ≤140/90 clinic
  • Check HBPM readings — target ≤135/85
  • Pharmacist to optimise and arrange follow-up
Not on statin?
  • Age ≥40, no CVD, no mod/severe frailty → check QRISK3
  • QRISK3 ≥10% → start atorvastatin 20 mg
  • CVD history → start atorvastatin 80 mg
  • If declined/unsuitable → alternative + document reason
