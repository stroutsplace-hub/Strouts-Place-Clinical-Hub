📋 QOF Indicators at a Glance
Code Indicator Points Target Scope
HF008 New diagnosis (on/after 1 Apr 2023) confirmed by echocardiogram or specialist assessment within 6 months before/after diagnosis, or within 6 months of registration 6 pts 50–90% All HF
HF003 Currently treated with ACE inhibitor or ARB 6 pts 60–92% HFrEF only
HF006 Currently treated with a beta-blocker licensed for heart failure 6 pts 60–92% HFrEF only
HF007 Annual review in preceding 12 months including functional capacity assessment and medication review at maximal tolerated doses 7 pts 50–90% All HF
ℹ️
HFrEF / LVSD — HF003 and HF006 apply only to patients with heart failure due to reduced ejection fraction. Ensure HF type is correctly coded in EMIS for every patient on the register.
💊 Drug Therapy — HFrEF / LVSD only
HF003 — ACE Inhibitor or ARB
  • First line: ACE inhibitor — titrate to maximum tolerated dose
  • If ACE-I intolerant (cough, angioedema): switch to ARB
  • Do not combine ACE-I and ARB
  • Check U&E and BP at initiation and each dose increase
  • If K⁺ >5.5 mmol/L or significant eGFR fall: reduce dose or escalate to GP
Ramipril Lisinopril Enalapril Perindopril
If ACE-I intolerant
Candesartan Valsartan Losartan
HF006 — Beta-blocker (licensed for HF)
  • Start at very low dose — titrate slowly over weeks to months
  • Symptoms may worsen initially — adjust diuretic if needed
  • Not withheld for: age, PVD, ED, DM, interstitial lung disease, COPD (no reversibility)
  • Contraindicated: asthma, reversible airways obstruction
  • Patients on unlicensed BB pre-diagnosis: excluded from HF006 — do not switch drug
✅ Licensed for HF
Bisoprolol Carvedilol Nebivolol (≥70 yrs)
🚫 Not licensed for HF
Atenolol Metoprolol Propranolol Sotalol
⚠️
Titration is mandatory — NICE NG106 requires upward titration to target or maximum tolerated dose. Document current dose and any clinical reason for not titrating at every review. Prescribing at a sub-therapeutic dose without documentation does not satisfy the clinical standard.
🩺 Physical Checks — HCA / Nurse (HF007 Annual Review)
Required at Every Annual Review
  • Functional capacity — NYHA class, exercise tolerance MUST BE CODED
  • Weight and fluid status — note any rapid gain
  • BMI
  • Blood pressure
  • Heart rate and pulse rhythm
  • Smoking status
  • Alcohol status (AUDIT-C)
  • Frailty and palliative / end-of-life care needs — consider where appropriate
Escalation from Review
  • Signs of worsening HF (↑ breathlessness, weight gain >2 kg in 3 days, oedema, new arrhythmia) → flag to GP same day
  • New AF, bradycardia <60, or unexplained tachycardia >100 → ECG and flag to GP same day
  • Any urgent clinical concern → GP same day — do not defer
📋
Use (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 — code all elements on the same day as the appointment.
NYHA Functional Classification
I
No limitation

Ordinary activity does not cause symptoms

II
Slight limitation

Comfortable at rest; symptoms with moderate exertion

III
Marked limitation

Comfortable at rest; symptoms with minimal activity

IV
Severe limitation

Symptoms at rest; unable to carry out any activity

⚠️
NYHA III or IV — consider referral to specialist HF team. NYHA IV with rapid deterioration — same-day GP review and consider emergency admission.
🧪 Monitoring Requirements
Investigation Frequency Key Actions / Notes Responsible
U&E
Renal function & electrolytes		 1–2 wks post change
6-monthly (stable) 		After initiation or dose increase of ACE-I, ARB or diuretic. Check for hyperkalaemia (K⁺ >5.5 mmol/L) and eGFR decline. Reduce dose or escalate to GP if abnormal. Pharmacist / GP
LFTs Annually Routine annual monitoring. More frequently if liver disease or amiodarone prescribed. Pharmacist / GP
FBC Annually Check for anaemia (common in HF, worsens prognosis) and iron deficiency. GP / Pharmacist
TFTs Annually Particularly important if on amiodarone. Thyroid dysfunction can worsen or precipitate HF. GP / Nurse
Blood pressure 1–2 wks post change
Each review 		At every clinical contact. After any antihypertensive or diuretic change. Target BP in HF is guided by clinical tolerance, not a fixed QOF threshold. HCA / Nurse / Pharmacist / GP
Heart rate & rhythm Each review At every structured review. ECG if new AF, significant bradycardia (<60), or unexplained tachycardia (>100). HCA / Nurse
Weight Each review Advise daily weighing at home. >2 kg gain in 3 days indicates fluid retention — adjust diuretic or contact practice per self-management plan. HCA / Nurse
NT-proBNP / BNP As indicated Not a routine annual requirement. Use at new / suspected diagnosis to guide referral urgency, or if clinical deterioration suspected. GP
💡
All monitoring results must be reviewed by a clinician and acted upon. Abnormal results must not be left without a documented clinical response.
📅 Review Frequency
Stable HF
  • Routine review every 6–12 months
  • Annual QOF review (HF007) must be within 12 months
After Medication Change
  • Follow-up within 1–2 weeks
  • Check BP, HR, symptoms and U&E
  • Review before further dose titration
Deteriorating / Complex
  • Shorten review interval — do not wait for annual review
  • Consider specialist HF team referral
  • Assess frailty and palliative / EOL needs
