Standard Operating Procedure

Stroke & TIA
QOF 2025/26

Achieving targets for STIA007, STIA014 & STIA015 — a guide for administrative and clinical staff at Strouts Place Medical Centre.

Strouts Place Medical Centre Version 1.0 Effective: April 2025 Review: March 2026 Owner: GP Lead
!

Why This Matters

⚠️
The Third Most Common Cause of Death in the Developed World Stroke is the third most common cause of death in the developed world — and one quarter of stroke deaths occur under the age of 65. Following a stroke or TIA, the risk of a further stroke is highest in the first few days and weeks. Evidence shows that appropriate ongoing management — including antiplatelet or anticoagulant therapy and sustained blood pressure control — significantly improves outcomes and reduces the risk of recurrence.
ℹ️
Scope All three indicators apply to patients on the Stroke/TIA register. This SOP covers the full annual review pathway — from register identification through to clinical review, medication review, BP optimisation, coding, and QOF monitoring. Note that patients on this register also appear within the scope of CHOL003 (statin prescribing) and CHOL004 (cholesterol target) — refer to the Cholesterol QOF SOP for those indicators.

💡
STIA007 — What counts? The medication must be confirmed as actively being taken — not merely prescribed. Qualifying therapies are set out below. Confirmation is completed by the Pharmacist at the medication review appointment.
💊  Antiplatelet & Anticoagulant Therapy — Clinical Guidance (STIA007)
Non-AF Ischaemic Stroke / TIA — First Line

Clopidogrel 75 mg daily is the preferred long-term treatment (note: unlicensed for TIA but recommended by NICE and BNF). Long-term antiplatelet therapy reduces the risk of serious vascular events by approximately a quarter.

Non-AF — If Clopidogrel Contra-indicated or Not Tolerated

Modified-release dipyridamole + aspirin. If both aspirin and clopidogrel are contra-indicated: MR dipyridamole alone. If both MR dipyridamole and clopidogrel are contra-indicated: aspirin alone.

Stroke Associated with AF

These patients should be reviewed for long-term treatment with warfarin or a DOAC (apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban, or dabigatran). Review anticoagulation status and confirm it is being actively taken. Anticoagulation counts for STIA007.

⚕️ Where therapy has been reviewed and declined or is clinically contraindicated, exception reporting is appropriate — but must be approved by the GP and clinically justified in the record.

Roles & Responsibilities

🗂️
Practice Manager
  • Owns overall QOF performance
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting; reviews dashboard and agrees action plans
  • Supports admin team with ongoing recall
  • Chairs escalation of persistent non-responders at the monthly QOF meeting
📋
Admin / Reception Team
  • Identifies register and sends invitation letters and SMS
  • Books annual review with HCA or Pharmacist
  • Scans and codes relevant secondary care correspondence on receipt
  • Logs DNA attempts; adds repeated non-responders to the monthly QOF meeting agenda
🩺
Healthcare Assistant (HCA)
  • Conducts annual Stroke/TIA review using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template in EMIS
  • Records blood pressure (clinic or HBPM) with method documented
  • Records weight, BMI, smoking status and alcohol (AUDIT-C)
  • Offers HBPM to patients with borderline or elevated clinic readings
  • Codes all findings on the day of the appointment
  • Flags uncontrolled BP to Pharmacist
💊
Pharmacist
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting
  • Conducts medication review
  • Confirms antiplatelet / anticoagulant therapy is being taken and codes STIA007
  • Reviews antihypertensive regimen; optimises BP for patients not at target
  • Ensures appropriate antiplatelet choice and reviews AF-related anticoagulation
  • Arranges monitoring investigations as appropriate (e.g. renal function, LFTs)
  • Runs Pharmacist BP clinic follow-up appointments as appropriate
  • Escalates to GP if clinical concerns are identified
👨‍⚕️
GP
  • Attends monthly QOF meeting; chairs clinical review
  • Acts on clinical concerns escalated by any clinician
  • Approves all exception reporting with documented clinical justification
  • Annual register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, verify haemorrhagic vs non-haemorrhagic coding, remove incorrect entries
  • Reviews patients with repeated DNA / declining attendance raised at the monthly QOF meeting

Step-by-Step Process

1
Identify Patients Due a Review
  • Identify patients with no review in the past 12 months or an outstanding indicator
  • Identify patients whose last BP reading exceeds the age-appropriate target (STIA014 / STIA015)
  • Identify patients with no antiplatelet / anticoagulant confirmed as taken in the past 12 months (STIA007)
  • Verify register accuracy: confirm stroke type coded as non-haemorrhagic (required for STIA007); STIA014 / STIA015 apply to all stroke and TIA patients — confirm age-band coding is correct
🧠
STIA007 register scope STIA007 applies only to patients whose stroke is coded as non-haemorrhagic (ischaemic), or who have a history of TIA. Patients with haemorrhagic stroke are excluded from STIA007 but remain on the register for the blood pressure indicators STIA014 and STIA015. The GP should verify haemorrhagic vs non-haemorrhagic coding at the annual register review.
2
Invite & Book
  • Admin identifies patients due a review and sends a personalised invitation letter or SMS
  • Book an annual Stroke/TIA review with HCA or Pharmacist
  • Advise patient to bring a list of current medications to the appointment
  • If no response after 2 weeks: send a second invitation. After a further 2 weeks: telephone outreach.
  • If still no response after further attempts: add to the monthly QOF meeting agenda for the team to review
3
Annual Stroke/TIA Review — HCA or Pharmacist

The annual review may be conducted by the HCA or Pharmacist using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template in EMIS. Everything must be coded on the same day.

🧠🩺  Annual Stroke/TIA review — all checks
  • ✓Blood pressure — clinic reading (STIA014 / STIA015)
  • ✓Weight & BMI
  • ✓Smoking status
  • ✓Alcohol status (AUDIT-C)
  • ✓Offer HBPM if clinic BP borderline or elevated
  • ✓All findings coded same day in EMIS
🔁  When to flag BP
⚠️  BP above target but <180/120

Flag to Pharmacist on the day. Admin to book a TC or F2F with the Pharmacist within 1 week.

🚨  BP ≥180/120

Do not book routine Pharmacist follow-up. Inform the GP same day for immediate discussion and review.

4
Medication Review & BP Optimisation — Pharmacist
  • STIA007: Confirm with the patient that antiplatelet or anticoagulant therapy is being actively taken and code the medication status. If therapy is not being taken and there is no recorded contraindication, review the clinical reasoning and escalate to the GP to initiate or review the prescription.
  • Antiplatelet choice review: Confirm the appropriate agent is prescribed per NICE NG128 / BNF guidance (see indicator section above). Where therapy appears suboptimal, discuss with the GP.
  • AF anticoagulation: For patients with stroke associated with AF, confirm a DOAC or warfarin is prescribed and actively being taken. Check INR is in range where relevant. Refer to the AF SOP if anticoagulation is suboptimal.
  • BP above target (STIA014 / STIA015): Review antihypertensive regimen and adherence; intensify therapy (add or up-titrate) as clinically appropriate; arrange Pharmacist BP clinic follow-up to recheck BP after medication change
  • Arrange monitoring investigations as appropriate — e.g. renal function, LFTs — particularly following medication initiation or dose change
  • Signpost to lifestyle interventions: smoking cessation, weight management, alcohol reduction, physical activity
  • Escalate to GP if clinical concerns are identified
🩺
Pharmacist BP Clinic Follow-up Where BP remains above the age-appropriate target following medication change, the Pharmacist schedules a dedicated BP clinic follow-up.
📋  Statin Therapy — Pharmacist awareness (CHOL003 / CHOL004)

Patients on the Stroke/TIA register are included within the scope of CHOL003 (statin prescribing) and CHOL004 (LDL ≤2.0 mmol/L or non-HDL ≤2.6 mmol/L). The Pharmacist should confirm statin therapy is prescribed at the medication review and refer to the Cholesterol QOF SOP for full guidance. High-intensity statins are preferred per the LES.

Where statin is declined or not tolerated, document the reason and consider an alternative lipid-lowering agent. Escalate to GP if clinically uncertain.

5
Coding & Documentation
  • All findings coded in EMIS on the day of the appointment using the (RP) LTC Review Tower Hamlets CEG v1.4 template
  • BP readings: use correct SNOMED codes and distinguish clinic readings from HBPM readings clearly
  • STIA007: antiplatelet / anticoagulant coded as being taken following Pharmacist confirmation at the medication review — not merely prescribed
  • Stroke type: ensure haemorrhagic vs non-haemorrhagic is correctly coded — this determines STIA007 eligibility and should be verified against secondary care records at registration
  • Secondary care correspondence: admin to scan and code relevant results and medication changes on receipt
  • Verify patient age band at each review — patients crossing the ≤79 / ≥80 threshold between cycles must be assigned to the correct indicator (STIA014 or STIA015)
6
Exception Reporting

Exceptions may be entered by any appropriate member of staff but must be approved by the GP, and only where clinically justified.

  • Patient has been informed of the recommendation but declines (patient declined)
  • Clinical contraindication documented and approved by the GP (e.g. antiplatelet therapy contraindicated due to high bleeding risk)
  • Patient recently registered (within 3 months) — new patient exception
  • Maximum tolerated antihypertensive therapy already in use and target not achievable
  • For STIA007: haemorrhagic stroke patients are excluded from the denominator and should not be exception-reported — verify coding rather than excepting
⚠️
Exception reporting must never be used to improve QOF scores without clinical justification. Commissioners may audit a random sample of exceptions — incorrect coding can result in financial claw-back.

Monitoring & QOF Meeting

📅
Monthly QOF Meeting The GP Lead, Pharmacist, Practice Manager and Admin Lead meet monthly to review the QOF dashboard, assess performance against STIA007, STIA014 and STIA015, problem-solve barriers and agree an actionable improvement plan. This meeting is also the forum for reviewing patients who have not responded to recall — the team agrees the approach collectively.
Frequency Activity Owner
Monthly QOF meeting — GP Lead, Pharmacist, PM and Admin Lead review Stroke/TIA dashboard, identify patients to recall, review non-responders and agree action plan GP Lead (Chair)
Monthly Admin team informed of patients to invite for annual review following QOF meeting Practice Manager / Admin Lead
Monthly Pharmacist reviews patients with BP above target — confirms Pharmacist BP clinic follow-up is booked Pharmacist
Monthly Pharmacist reviews patients with no antiplatelet / anticoagulant confirmed as taken (STIA007) — determines action: recall, exception, or GP review Pharmacist
As needed Patients with DNA or declining attendance reviewed at monthly QOF meeting — team agrees approach; GP involved if clinical concerns QOF Meeting
Annually Full register accuracy review — add new diagnoses, verify haemorrhagic vs non-haemorrhagic coding, validate age-band splits, remove incorrect entries GP Lead
